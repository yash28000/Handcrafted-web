Meet the Heroes

Meet Bhavnaben :


Bhavnaben Bhikabhai Mochi


Age- 39
Children- 4
Community- Mochi
Hobbies- Watching television,
practising Aari, spending time with
family



Bhavnaben learned about the Aari in her
leisure time. After managing the
home and sending her kids to school
she spent her time practising the
craft. When we met her, she
demonstrated the process of Aari,
taught us the chain stitch technique
along with her daughter and we
practised it with her.

Meet Bhikabhai :


Bhikabhai Mochi


Age- 39
Children- 4
Community- Mochi
Hobbies- Watching television,
practising Aari, spending time with
family



Bhikabhai was born into the family of artisans,
his father being a Mochi artisan. Though his life
took several turns in his professional life,
switching careers, he found his way back to Aari
embroidery and I one of main Aari employer of
the artisans in Diyodar. He wishes to keep this
beautiful craft alive while also helping 500+
women gain a livelihood by employing them.

Meet Umniyaben :


Umniyaben


Age- 43
Children- 2
Community- Mochi
Hobbies- Watching television,
practising Aari, spending time with
family



Umniyaben is a housemaker who utilizes her
leisure time working at the organisation SEWA.
SEWA is an organisation that connects artisans
to designers where they are delegated work.
Umniyaben has been practising Aari embroidery
for many years and has dedicated her life to the
craft. She has worked on a wide variety of
clothing pieces and has mastered
the craft of Aari.
